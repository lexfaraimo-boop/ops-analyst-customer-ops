The problem

Get things built, but not maintained

🚚
Projects get delivered and handed over. Handed over to a team that does not do these tasks in their regular jobs — these maintenance tasks should mostly stay with an operations person.
📉
Systems go stale, processes break with other changes. Small issues compound without a clear owner to catch them.
🔄
Daily ops has no permanent home. The recurring operational work the customer team depends on has no dedicated, consistent owner.
2 / 6
The solution

The maintenance side of the ops function

This role closes the gap. Right now, our analysts are project-based — this is the maintenance equivalent of that. Someone whose entire focus is the customer ops layer: keeping it running, keeping it accurate, and making sure nothing drifts.

Not project work — maintenance, iteration, and continuity of everything already live
Wakes up every day thinking about the customer ops layer — spotting improvements and areas that will need change
Brings those findings to operations and the customer team for review — not acting alone, but keeping the right people informed
3 / 6
What they own

Live systems. Daily ops. Everything running.

⚙️

Live Systems Maintenance

  • Maintaining tools, automations, and workflows already in production
  • Monitoring for breakage, drift, and degradation
  • Keeping data flows accurate and up to date
📋

Daily Operational Processes

  • Running the recurring ops that the Customer & AM team depends on
  • Process consistency and quality across the cycle
  • First line of triage for ops issues
📈

Improvements & Iteration

  • Incremental improvements to what already exists
  • Spotting inefficiencies and fixing them with team input
  • Continuation of prior work — no starting from scratch
📚

Documentation & Continuity

  • Keeping process documentation current and accurate
  • Ensuring institutional knowledge doesn't walk out the door
  • Supporting onboarding into the Customer & AM ops layer
🚫

Not net-new builds. This role is sustaining and iterating, not scoping new projects. New builds stay with Core Ops. This person owns what's live.

Why they're in Ops
They run the processes

Cross-functional awareness
