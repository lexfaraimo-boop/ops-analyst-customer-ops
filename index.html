The problem

Things get built — but not maintained

🚚
Projects get delivered and handed over. Once something is built, it lands with the team — but no one in ops owns it after that point.
📉
Things drift over time. Systems go stale, processes break quietly, and small issues compound without a clear owner to catch them.
🔄
Daily ops has no permanent home. The recurring operational work the Customer and AM team depends on has no dedicated, consistent owner.
2 / 6
The solution

A dedicated analyst who owns the layer

This role closes the gap. An operationally-minded analyst whose entire focus is keeping the Customer and AM operational layer running — not building new things, but making sure everything live keeps working.

Permanent ownership of what already exists — no more handover with no home
Someone who wakes up every day thinking about the Customer & AM ops layer
Continuity, not project work — sustaining, iterating, and catching drift before it compounds
3 / 6
What they own

Live systems. Daily ops. Everything running.

⚙️

Live Systems Maintenance

  • Maintaining tools, automations, and workflows already in production
  • Monitoring for breakage, drift, and degradation
  • Keeping data flows accurate and up to date
📋

Daily Operational Processes

  • Running the recurring ops that the Customer & AM team depends on
  • Process consistency and quality across the cycle
  • First line of triage for ops issues
📈

Improvements & Iteration

  • Incremental improvements to what already exists
  • Spotting inefficiencies and fixing them with team input
  • Continuation of prior work — no starting from scratch
📚

Documentation & Continuity

  • Keeping process documentation current and accurate
  • Ensuring institutional knowledge doesn't walk out the door
  • Supporting onboarding into the Customer & AM ops layer
🚫

Not net-new builds. This role is sustaining and iterating, not scoping new projects. New builds stay with Core Ops. This person owns what's live.
